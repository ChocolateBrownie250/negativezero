Six directions for the main page.

Each one is a self-contained sketch — same content, different posture. Open them, see which one sounds like you. We can keep one wholesale, or steal bits from each.

01 / editorial minimal

The Footnote

Two-column editorial layout. Wide reading column on the left, marginalia on the right for paradox notes and definitions. Long-form, considered, magazine-like.

paper white · marginalia · long-form
02 / structured catalogue

The Index

Numbered, list-based, mono-heavy. Everything is an entry. Bio is three labelled lines. Projects are a table with IDs and tags. Personal cabinet of curiosities.

geist mono · numbered · dashed rules
03 / spirograph (dark)

The Hypotrochoid

Minimal-text dark layout led by an animation: a small circle rolls inside a large one, the pen traces a seven-petalled hypotrochoid. Construction lines fade, gold flower stays.

dark · spirograph · pure aesthetic
04 / koch limit (dark)

The Snowflake

Same minimal shell, but the entrance morphs a triangle through Koch iterations n = 0 → 6, ending at n → ∞. Edges and perimeter diverge, area stays finite.

dark · fractal · limit + ∞
05 / taylor series (dark)

The Approximation

Real KaTeX-typeset equation grows term by term while the polynomial approximation hugs sin(x) more tightly. Settles on the infinite-series form. The calculus-textbook one.

dark · KaTeX · convergence
06 / lorenz attractor (dark)

The Butterfly

Not a graph of a function. Builds the Lorenz attractor live in a plot-framed canvas; the ODE system appears below in KaTeX. Pure visual, calculus reference.

dark · ODE · phase plot · aesthetic
